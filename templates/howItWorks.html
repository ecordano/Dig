How it Works

Our algorithm uses research about the top 22 most common crops in the NorthEast and the different factors required for each to flourish i.e. amount of sun, water, nutritional value, how much land one plant occupies, etc. We then developed a process to convert the units and normalize the data. From there, we had the basic constraints of sunlight, water, land, and labor. To make the model more personalized, we included a way for users to rank the preferences for their crops: the higher the preference, the more likely the program would be to suggest the crop. As we further considered how to get users the best choice of crops, we decided to allow them to choose between maximizing profit or maximizing nutritional yield. As a result, the user now has a great deal of control over the program and the associated outputs.
