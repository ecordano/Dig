Applying for Land

If you have a lot in mind, great! If not, you can search for one here. If you would like to begin gardening without receiving a permit, garden at your own risk! The city may take the land at any given time.


A safer and recommended alternative would be to aply for a Garden Agreement License. With this, the city can still terminate the lease at any given time, but they generally tend to respect the growing season. Along these lines, you can also secure a Community Garden Lease for up to 5 years. This option is more permanent but you will need to obtain a general liability insurance policy.


If you live close to the lot in question, it may be possible to garden the property as part of the Dollar Side Lot Program.


Finally, you may purchase the property for a discounted price or nominal fee by contacting your district councilperson.
